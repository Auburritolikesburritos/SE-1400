Cats

Charateristics of a cat

  • Indepedent, cats enjoy attention but also dont rely on humans for constant attention, most cats atleast
  • Curious, cats love exploring, solving problems, and investigating
  • Agile, cats can jump up to 6 times their body length, balance on narrow areas and move with precision
  • Affectionate, but only on their own terms, they only want to be pet when they feel like it
  • Playful, most cats are playful and like to chase, pounce, and climb

This pet would suit you if...

  • Someone who needs a low maintence companion, cats dont require walks or constant attention
  • Has a busy schedule, cats can handle alone time better than most pets
  • Lives in a small space or apartment, cats dont need alot of room and enjoy small places
  • Someone who wants a quieter pet, most cats are more quiet and relaxed
  • Doesnt like grooming them, cats are self groomers

Costs of this pet

  • Food, $20-$40 a month, depending on what kind of food you buy and how healthy it is
  • Litter, $10-$30 a month, depends on the kind you like to buy
  • Routine medications, $10-$25 a month, like flea/tick medication or deworming
  • Pet insurance, $15-$40 a month, depends on the age and breed
  • Toys and supplies, $5-$40, includes toys and treats

Cats would not be ideal for someone...

  • Wants a highly affectionte pet, cats are more indepedent
  • Wants a obedient pet, most cats arent good listeners, cats dont follow commands like dogs do
  • Dislikes litter boxes, having an indoor cat means keeping up with the litter box and cleaning it
  • Has an allergy to fur
  • Wakes easily, cats get the zoomies at night and can be loud
  • Has a lot of plants, there are plenty of plants that can easily be toxic to cats and may kill them if ingested

Fun facts about cats

  • Cats have a built in GPS, they have a powerful homing instinct, they can find there way back home from long distances
  • Cats cant taste sweets, cats lack taste receptors for sweet flavors
  • Cats ears are very flexible, cats have 32 muscles in their ears and rotate them up to 180 degrees to pinpoint sounds
  • A group of cats is called a clowder and a group of kittens is called a kindle
  • Cats spend 70% of their time sleeping
  • Purring has healing powers, cats purr at frequencies 25-150 Hz shown to promote healing of bones and tissue
  • Cats groom to relax, its not just hygiene it helps them reduce stress and regulate body temperature
  • Cats dream
  • Along with dogs, cats noses are all unique just like human fingerprints
