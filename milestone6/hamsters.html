Hamsters

Charateristics of a hamster

  • Hamsters are small in size, most hamsters are 2-7 inches long so they dont take up that much room!
  • Nocturnal, hamsters are nocturnal creatures meaning they sleep during the day and are active at night
  • Gentle, most hamsters are generally very friendly and tame
  • Startled easily, sudden noises or waiting them up all of a sudden can stress them out

This pet would suit you if...

  • You needed a more low maintenance pet, hamsters dont need constant attention, good for people who dont want a demanding pet
  • Great for indepedent people, hamsters prefer doing their own thing unlike dogs or cats
  • Good for someone who has limited space, good for apartments or small living spaces

Costs of this pet

  • Bedding, $5-$15 per month, hamsters need clean absorbent bedding, a full bedding change is needed every 2-4 weeks
  • Food, food costs $5-$10 per month, one bag of good hamster food should last about 4-8weeks
  • Chew toys/Enrichment, hamsters constantly need something to chew on to keep their teeth healthy, they need toys to avoid boredom
  • One time costs can include a cage, roughly around $40-$100, a hamster wheel, $15-$30, waterbottle and bowls, $20-$50 dollars

Hamsters would not be ideal for someone...

  • Wants a pet that interacts with them often
  • Is sensitive to nighttime noise, hamsters like to play during the night and sleep during the day
  • Wants a long living pet, hamsters only live up to 2-3 years
  • Is young and might handle the hamster roughly, hamsters are very delicate and can get injured or die very easily, they can also bite when scared

Fun facts about hamsters

  • Hamsters can store up tp 20% of their body weight in their cheek pouches
  • Hamster run A LOT, they can run up to 5-6 miles a single night
  • Their teeth never stop growing, this is why hamsters need to be constantly chewing on something, to keep their teeth from growing to long
  • Hamsters hoard food in hidden spots
  • A specific type of hamster, the syrian hamster, has to be housed alone due to territorial and fighting issues
  • Hamsters can run backwards just as easy as running fowards, they have a super flexible spine
